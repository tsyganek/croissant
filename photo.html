10 Facts About Croissants 🥐

  1. Croissants were born not in France, but in Austria, and date back to the end of 17th century. Croissants are descendants of a pastry named kipfel which is a kind of bread roll known since 13th century.

  2. In France, croissants appeared in the 19th century. Maria Antoinette 👸, the queen of France, introduced this treat to the royal kitchen to be baked just for her. In public, croissants appeared in Paris in Boulangerie Vennoise. A bakery under the same name still exists in the capital of France, but it is not the mother of croissants.

  3. The most important ingredient in croissants is unsalted butter, which is spread on the dough layer by layer before baking. This is what gives the croissant its signature buttery flavor.

  4. A classic French croissant has 55 layers (27 layers of butter), achieved with a French fold followed by 3 letter folds.

  5. Croissant dough needs to rest for 24 hours before being baked.

  6. Nineteenth-century croissants had 2 sizes (1 sou, 36-37 grams, and 2 sous, 60-80 grams).

  7. In 1920, the croissant was awarded the place of the national product of France🇫🇷.

  8. The USA and Canada have a special day celebrating the existence of croissants. So, the National Croissant Day in the USA is January 30.

  9. French croissants have relatives in other European cuisines: German🇩🇪 laugencroissants, Italian🇮🇹 cornettos, Polish🇵🇱 rogals świętomarciński, and gipfels from Switzerland🇨🇭.

  10. Starting out as a food item for nobles, in the beginning, the dish soon became a dish relished by everyone.
